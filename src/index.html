A View of Places

African Renaissance Monument

Senegal

It is Africa's largest statue and a major sight for Dakar. Huge, colossal really at some 22,000 tons, built of shining bronze constructed on the Mamelles hill, which adds a hundred meters to its already 49metre height.

Bo Kaap

South Africa

Known for its brightly coloured homes and cobble stoned streets, many tourist guides or blogs will find a reason for these colours, Inhabitants simply wanted to give a joyful look to the neighbourhood with paint of vivid colours.

Chefchaouen

Morocco

Nicknamed the “blue pearl of Morocco”, Nestled in the Rif Mountains of Northern Morocco, There are various theories as to why the city was painted blue. The Blue City of Morocco really is as pretty as you see on Instagram.

Nabiyotum Crater

Kenya

Kenya's famous and beautiful crater, located next to another prominent landmark, Lake Turkana(the largest alkaline lake in the world). It’s a caldera the remnants of a collapsed volcano. Traveling here is not for the meek.

Praslin

Seychelles

The second largest of the Seychelles archipelago, In a certain Pirate’s Cove on Praslin. It is believed that a famous pirate hid a treasure worth more than $160,000 still unfound.

Pyramid Of Giza

Egypt

The oldest and only remaining of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, it was the tallest structure in the world for about 4000years until modern skyscrapers.

Sahara Dunes (Erg Chebbi)

Morocco

The Sahara desert has a variety of land features, but is most famous for the sand dune fields. The dunes can reach almost 600 feet (183 meters) high and they cover about 15 percent of the entire desert.

Sossusvlei Dunes

Namibia

This is a perfect specimen of unspoilt desert beauty. A salt and clay pan in the largest conservation area in Africa, the Namib-Naukluft National Park. It is famous for its large, red sand dunes, some of the world's tallest sand dunes.

The Great Migration

Tanzania

A photo won't capture the thunder of hooves on dirt as more than a million wildebeest and several hundred thousand zebra make their annual migration, but still, this is one for the top of the pile.

Tsingy de Bemaraha National Park

Madagascar

The word Tsingy is a Malagasy word that can loosely be translated as walking on tiptoes and this is the due to the spiky nature of the several surroundings of the Park that were formed from the spiky limestones many years back.

Victoria Falls

Zambia and Zimbabwe

The approach being signaled only by the mighty roar and veil of mist for which the Kalolo-Lozi people named it Mosi-oa-Tunya (“The Smoke That Thunders”). 0ne of the world's largest waterfalls.

Yankari Game Reserve

Nigeria

Its location in the heartland of the West African savanna makes it a unique way for tourists to watch wildlife in its natural habitat. Home to over 50 species of mammals and some 350 species of birds.
